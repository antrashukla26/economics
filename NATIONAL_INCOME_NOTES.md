# National Income Notes

## Introduction
National income is a measure of the total economic activity of a nation, representing the monetary value of all finished goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific time period.

## Circular Flow of Income
The circular flow model illustrates how money moves through an economy, highlighting the interactions between households, firms, and the government.

1. **Households** provide factors of production (labor, capital) to firms in exchange for wages and income.
2. **Firms** produce goods and services using the factors of production and sell them to households.
3. **Government** collects taxes and injects money back into the economy through public spending.

## Methodologies for Measuring National Income
National income can be estimated using three primary methods:

1. **Production Method** (Output): Measures the total value added by industries.
2. **Income Method**: Adds up all incomes earned by factors of production.
3. **Expenditure Method**: Totals consumption, investment, government spending, and net exports.
